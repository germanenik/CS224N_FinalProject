# transparency report

apple is committed to your privacy and being transparent about government
requests for customer data globally. [CLS] [SEP] this report provides information on
government requests received. [CLS] [SEP]

  * ## device

device requests are based on device identifiers such as apple serial number,
imei or meid. [CLS] [SEP]

device requests generally seek information regarding customers associated with
devices and device connections to apple services - for example, law
enforcement investigations on behalf of customers regarding lost or stolen
devices. [CLS] [SEP]

additionally, apple regularly receives multi-device requests related to fraud
investigations. [CLS] [SEP]

[view device requests](/legal/transparency/device-requests.html)

### device requests by year - worldwide

  * ## financial identifier

financial identifier requests are based on financial identifiers such as
credit/debit card or itunes gift card. [CLS] [SEP]

financial identifier requests generally seek information regarding suspected
fraudulent transactions - for example, law enforcement investigations on
behalf of customers in which a credit card was fraudulently used to purchase
apple products or services. [CLS] [SEP]

apple began reporting on financial identifier requests in transparency report
period july 1 - december 31, 2016. [CLS] [SEP]

[view financial identifier requests](/legal/transparency/financial-
identifier.html)

### financial identifier requests by year - worldwide

  * ## account

account requests are based on account identifiers such as an apple id or email
address. [CLS] [SEP]  
  
account requests generally seek information regarding customers’ apple id
accounts, such as account holder name and address and account connections to
apple services - for example, law enforcement investigations where an account
may have been used unlawfully. [CLS] [SEP] account requests may also seek customers’
content data, such as photos, email, ios device backups, contacts or
calendars. [CLS] [SEP]  

[view account requests](/legal/transparency/account.html)

### account requests by year - worldwide

  * ## account preservation

account preservation requests are based on account identifiers such as an
apple id or email address. [CLS] [SEP]  

account preservation requests ask apple to preserve a one-time copy of
customer account data - for example, law enforcement investigations where an
account may have been used unlawfully, and apple is requested to preserve
account data while a valid legal request for the data is obtained. [CLS] [SEP]  

apple began reporting on account preservation requests in transparency report
period july 1 - december 31, 2016. [CLS] [SEP]

[view account preservation requests](/legal/transparency/account-
preservation.html)

  * ## account restriction/deletion

account restriction/deletion requests ask apple to restrict or delete a
customer’s account, for example, law enforcement determines an account was
used unlawfully and obtains a court order to restrict or delete the account. [CLS] [SEP]

apple began reporting on account restriction requests in transparency report
period july 1 - december 31, 2016. [CLS] [SEP]

apple began reporting on account deletion requests in transparency report
period july 1 - december 31, 2014. [CLS] [SEP]  

[view account restriction/deletion requests](/legal/transparency/account-
restriction.html)

### account restriction/deletion requests by year - worldwide

  * ## emergency

emergency requests relate to circumstances involving imminent danger of death
or serious physical injury to any person, and generally seek information
regarding customers’ connections to apple services - for example, instances
where law enforcement believe a person is missing and in danger. [CLS] [SEP]  

apple began reporting on emergency requests as a separate category in
transparency report period january 1 - june 30, 2015. [CLS] [SEP]  prior to this,
emergency requests were reported in applicable account and/or device request
categories. [CLS] [SEP]

[view emergency requests](/legal/transparency/emergency-requests.html)

### emergency requests by year - worldwide

  * ## united states national security

u.s. [CLS] [SEP] national security requests seek customer data in response to national
security related investigations. [CLS] [SEP] national security requests include orders
received under the foreign intelligence surveillance act (“fisa”) and national
security letters (“nsls”). [CLS] [SEP] to date, apple has not received any orders for bulk
data. [CLS] [SEP]

apple reports national security requests received for apple users/accounts
(nsls and orders received under fisa) within bands permissible by law pursuant
to the usa freedom act of 2015 (“usa freedom”). [CLS] [SEP] though we want to be more
specific, these are currently the ranges and level of detail permitted under
usa freedom for reporting u.s. [CLS] [SEP] national security requests. [CLS] [SEP]  

[view u.s. [CLS] [SEP] national security requests](/legal/transparency/us.html)

## u.s. [CLS] [SEP] national security requests by period

## report period

h1 represents january 1 through june 30. [CLS] [SEP]

h2 represents july 1 through december 31. [CLS] [SEP]

##

## request type

national security requests: includes fisa court issued orders for non-content
and content data as well as national security letters issued by the federal
bureau of investigation. [CLS] [SEP]  
  
fisa non-content & content requests: fisa court issued orders for non-content
or content data. [CLS] [SEP] non-content data is data such as subscriber or transactional
information and connection logs. [CLS] [SEP] content data is data such as stored photos,
email, ios device backups, contacts or calendars. [CLS] [SEP]  
  
national security letters (nsls): federal bureau of investigation issued
requests for non-content data in national security investigations. [CLS] [SEP] non-content
data is data such as subscriber data. [CLS] [SEP] apple does not produce transactional
information and connection logs in response to national security letters. [CLS] [SEP]

##

## requests received

the number of united states national security requests received. [CLS] [SEP]

for report periods 2013 h1 - 2017 h2, we count each individual request
received and report the total number of requests received within bands/ranges
permissible by law. [CLS] [SEP]  
  
for report periods 2018 h1 and subsequent, we count each individual order and
national security letter received for apple users/accounts and report the
total number of orders and national security letters received within
bands/ranges permissible by law. [CLS] [SEP] in order to report fisa non-content and
content requests in separate categories, we are required by law, under the usa
freedom act, to delay reporting by 6 months and report in bands/ranges of 500. [CLS] [SEP]

reporting the number of requests received for the most current reporting
period may be subject to this legally required 6-month reporting delay. [CLS] [SEP]

##

## users/accounts

the number of accounts where apple provided data in response to united states
national security requests. [CLS] [SEP] we count the number of accounts where we provide
data and report the total number of accounts affected within bands/ranges
permissible by law. [CLS] [SEP] reporting the number of users/accounts affected for the
most current reporting period may be subject to a legally required 6-month
reporting delay pursuant to usa freedom act of 2015. [CLS] [SEP]

  * ## united states private party

u.s. [CLS] [SEP] private party (non-government) requests generally seek information about
connections to apple services, purchase transactions, or product registrations
relating to an apple customer - for example, civil or criminal litigation case
proceedings where a subpoena is issued for information. [CLS] [SEP]

apple began reporting on u.s. [CLS] [SEP] private party requests in transparency report
period july 1 - december 31, 2016. [CLS] [SEP]

[view u.s. [CLS] [SEP] private party requests](/legal/transparency/us.html)

### u.s. [CLS] [SEP] private party requests by year

  * ## app removal

app removal requests ask apple to remove an app from the app store. [CLS] [SEP]  these
requests can be based on alleged/suspected violations of local law and/or of
app store platform policies. [CLS] [SEP]  for example, law enforcement or regulatory
agencies suspect an app may be unlawful or relate to/contain unlawful content,
or may violate the app store platform policies or relate to/contain content
violating platform policies. [CLS] [SEP]  

apple began reporting on app removal requests in transparency report period
july 1 - december 31, 2018. [CLS] [SEP]  

[view app removal requests - legal violation](/legal/transparency/app-removal-
requests-legal-violation.html)

[view app removal requests - platform violation](/legal/transparency/app-
removal-requests-platform-violation.html)
