##  cookies & other technologies

this policy explains our use of cookies and other technologies to understand,
improve and protect kickstarter. [CLS] [SEP] if you see a capitalized word, it’s defined
in our [terms of use](/terms-of-use). [CLS] [SEP]

### what they are

cookies are small data files that are issued to your device when you visit a
website and that store information about your use of a service. [CLS] [SEP] pixel tags
(which are also called clear gifs, web beacons, or pixels) are little snippets
of code or tiny images embedded on websites and in email that help us learn
how you interact with our site and emails. [CLS] [SEP]

### why we use them

kickstarter uses cookies and these other technologies to help recognize you as
a repeat visitor and, to improve the quality of our services. [CLS] [SEP]

### how they work

when you visit kickstarter, our web server sends a cookie to your device,
which allows us to recognize your device (but not the specific person using
it). [CLS] [SEP] by associating the identification numbers in the cookies with other
account information when, for example, you log in to our services, we know
that the cookie information relates to your user account. [CLS] [SEP] similarly, pixel
tags on kickstarter and in email communications help us identify how your
device is being used to interact with kickstarter content. [CLS] [SEP] some of the tags
and cookies used by our services are served by us, and some are served by
trusted partners who are delivering services on our behalf. [CLS] [SEP]

some examples of the types of cookies and other technologies kickstarter uses:

  * #### authentication

kickstarter uses authentication cookies and similar technologies to tell us
when you’re logged in to kickstarter. [CLS] [SEP] this lets us show you personalized views
related to your interests and connect you with projects like those you may
already have backed. [CLS] [SEP]

  * #### security

cookies also help keep kickstarter secure by facilitating security features
and allowing us to detect activity that might violate our rules and [terms of
use](/terms-of-use). [CLS] [SEP]

these cookies help protect your account from being accessed by anyone other
than you, alert you and us when your account is accessed, and provide
capabilities that allow us to disable any active sessions you have (for
example, when you log out or change your password). [CLS] [SEP]

  * #### localization

some cookies help us provide localized experiences — for example, by making
sure you see kickstarter in your preferred language. [CLS] [SEP]

  * #### site features and services

kickstarter uses cookies that provide functionality and help us deliver our
products and services — for instance, by storing your preferences or by pre-
filling the username field when you log in. [CLS] [SEP]

  * #### performance

performance cookies help us route traffic between servers and understand how
kickstarter is performing, so we can provide you with the best experience
possible. [CLS] [SEP] we may use third party software development kits (sdk) in our mobile
apps to collect information about activity in the app, the type of device and
operating system it is running on and how the app is functioning. [CLS] [SEP] for example
an sdk may send us a report if the app crashes, and reports on which features
of the app are used more than others. [CLS] [SEP]

  * #### analytics and research

cookies and other technologies also help us to understand, improve, and
research features and content on the kickstarter site. [CLS] [SEP] for example, we may use
cookies to understand how you are discovering projects or to determine which
types of browsers or devices are accessing kickstarter. [CLS] [SEP] you can opt-out of
being included in google analytics
[here](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout). [CLS] [SEP]

  * #### social media platforms

cookies and other technologies make interacting with social media platforms
more seamless. [CLS] [SEP] for example, when you’re signed into social media accounts
while you use our services, these technologies enable you to share content
with your social network or, in some cases, log in using your social media
credentials. [CLS] [SEP] these features are usually controlled by the social media
platform you are using and are governed by its separate privacy policies and
the preferences you set with that service. [CLS] [SEP]

  * #### marketing

cookies help us deliver targeted advertisements on social media platforms,
based on your activity on kickstarter, including the projects you’ve backed or
creators you’ve followed on kickstarter, and to track the performance of those
ads. [CLS] [SEP] this is sometimes referred to as online behavioral advertising. [CLS] [SEP] in some
cases, our partners may use cookies to provide us with information about your
interactions with their services. [CLS] [SEP] those third-party cookies would be subject
to the third party service’s policies. [CLS] [SEP]

you can opt-out of online behavioral advertising. [CLS] [SEP] the self-regulatory program
for online behavioral advertising program provides consumers with the ability
to opt-out of having their online behavior recorded and used for advertising
purposes. [CLS] [SEP] to opt out of having your online behavior collected for advertising
purposes, click [here](http://optout.aboutads.info/?c=2#!/). [CLS] [SEP]

certain choices you make are both browser- and device-specific. [CLS] [SEP]

### you can control certain cookies

your browser may give you the ability to control cookies. [CLS] [SEP] how you do so
depends on the type of cookie. [CLS] [SEP] certain browsers can be set to reject browser
cookies. [CLS] [SEP] blocking or deleting cookies may prevent you from using most of our
services. [CLS] [SEP] to find out more about how to enable, disable, or delete cookies
from your web browser, please visit [here](http://www.allaboutcookies.org/). [CLS] [SEP]
to control flash cookies, which may be used on certain websites from time to
time, you can go
[here](http://www.macromedia.com/support/documentation/en/flashplayer/help/settings_manager07.html). [CLS] [SEP]
why? because flash cookies cannot be controlled through your browser settings. [CLS] [SEP]

### our do not track policy

some browsers have “do not track” features that allow you to tell a website
not to track you. [CLS] [SEP] these features are not all uniform. [CLS] [SEP] we do not currently
respond to those signals. [CLS] [SEP] if you block cookies, certain features on our sites
may not work. [CLS] [SEP] if you block or reject cookies, not all of the tracking
described here will stop. [CLS] [SEP]

certain options you select are browser- and device-specific. [CLS] [SEP]
