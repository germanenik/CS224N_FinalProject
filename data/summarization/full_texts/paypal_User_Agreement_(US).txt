#### significantly not as described claims

  * the item was damaged during shipment. [CLS] [SEP]
  * the item is unusable in its received state and was not disclosed as such. [CLS] [SEP]

  * the defect in the item was correctly described by the seller in its description of the item. [CLS] [SEP]
  * the item was properly described but you didn't want it after you received it. [CLS] [SEP]

### paypal's seller protection program

#### consumer fraud warning

we're always looking for ways to help keep you even more secure. [CLS] [SEP] so stay on
the lookout for some of these common scams:

    * spoofing: a scammer sends forged or faked electronic documents or emails falsely claiming to be paypal or coming from paypal but asks you to send money outside your paypal account. [CLS] [SEP]
    * relative in need: a scammer impersonates a family member (commonly grandchildren) and claims there is an emergency requiring you to send money. [CLS] [SEP]
    * lottery or prize: a scammer informs you that you've won a lottery or prize and must send money in order to claim it. [CLS] [SEP]
    * debt collection: a scammer impersonates a debt collector and induces you to send money using threats or intimidation. [CLS] [SEP]
    * employment related: a scammer instructs you to send money in connection with a fraudulent offer of employment. [CLS] [SEP]

always use common sense when sending money. [CLS] [SEP] if something sounds too good to be
true, it probably is. [CLS] [SEP] only send money for yourself and not for others. [CLS] [SEP]
remember that if you don't send the payment through your paypal account,
you're not covered by paypal purchase protection. [CLS] [SEP] please let us know
immediately if you believe someone is trying to scam or defraud you by
[contacting us](https://www.paypal.com/us/smarthelp/home). [CLS] [SEP]
