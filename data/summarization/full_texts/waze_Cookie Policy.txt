# waze cookie policy

this policy explains how waze mobile limited and its affiliates (“waze”) uses
cookies and your related choices. [CLS] [SEP] for more information on other ways we
collect and use information from you and your use of our services, please see
our [privacy policy](/legal/privacy). [CLS] [SEP] in this policy we use the term “cookies”
to refer to any applicable tracking technologies described herein. [CLS] [SEP]

## what are cookies?

  * **a cookie** is a small piece of text sent to your browser by a website you visit. [CLS] [SEP] it helps the website to remember information about your visit, like your preferred language and other settings. [CLS] [SEP] that can make your next visit easier and the site more useful to you. [CLS] [SEP] cookies play an important role. [CLS] [SEP] without them, using the web would be a much more frustrating experience. [CLS] [SEP]
  * **a pixel tag** is a type of technology placed on a website or within the body of an email for the purpose of tracking certain activity, such as views of a website or when an email is opened. [CLS] [SEP] pixel tags are often used in combination with cookies. [CLS] [SEP]
  * **single sign-on** allows you to log in to certain of websites or mobile applications using sign-in services provided by third parties, such as facebook or google. [CLS] [SEP] these services will authenticate your identity and provide you the option to share certain personal information with us such as your name and email address to pre-populate our [sign-up form](/signin?redirect=%2f). [CLS] [SEP]

## how we use cookies?

**below you can see a list of the types of cookies used by waze. [CLS] [SEP] you can also
find out how waze and our partners use cookies in advertising.**

### processes

process cookies help the website to work and deliver services that the website
visitor expects, like navigating around web pages or accessing secure areas of
the website. [CLS] [SEP] without these cookies, the website cannot function properly. [CLS] [SEP]

### preferences

these cookies allow our websites to remember information that changes the way
the site behaves or looks, such as the region you are in. [CLS] [SEP] for instance, by
remembering your region, a website may be able to provide you with relevant
language preferences. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies can also assist you in changing text size,
font, and other parts of web pages that you can personalize. [CLS] [SEP]

loss of the information stored in a preference cookie may make the website
experience less functional but should not prevent it from working. [CLS] [SEP]

### authentication

we use these cookies to help us show you the right information and settings
when you are logged-in to our website. [CLS] [SEP]

### security

we use security cookies to authenticate users, prevent fraudulent use of login
credentials, and protect user data from unauthorized parties. [CLS] [SEP]

### session state

websites often collect information about how users interact with a website. [CLS] [SEP]
this may include the pages users visit most often and whether users get error
messages from certain pages. [CLS] [SEP] we use these so-called ‘session state cookies’ to
help us improve our services, in order to improve our users’ browsing
experience. [CLS] [SEP] blocking or deleting these cookies will not render the website
unusable. [CLS] [SEP]

### advertising

depending on your setting, we permit third-party ad partners (see a list
below) to use cookies and other technology from this site to market our
services to you on other websites and online services you visit. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies
also may help to limit the number of times that you see an ad and help us
measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. [CLS] [SEP]

ad partners:

  * [google](https://adssettings.google.com/authenticated?hl=en). [CLS] [SEP]
  * [facebook](https://www.facebook.com/ads/preferences/?entry_product=ad_settings_screen). [CLS] [SEP]
  * [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/start/join?session_redirect=https%3a%2f%2fwww.linkedin.com%2fpsettings%2fadvertising%2fwebsites-visited&trk=login_reg_redirect). [CLS] [SEP]
  * [snapchat](https://support.snapchat.com/en-us/a/advertising-preferences). [CLS] [SEP]
  * [yahoo](https://aim.yahoo.com/aim/us/en/optout/?guccounter=1). [CLS] [SEP]
  * [twitter](https://twitter.com/settings/personalization). [CLS] [SEP]

learn more about how you can control [advertising
cookies](/account/cookies_preferences). [CLS] [SEP]

### analytics

google analytics is google’s analytics tool that helps website and app owners
to understand how their visitors engage with their properties. [CLS] [SEP] it may use a
set of cookies to collect information and report website usage statistics
without personally identifying individual visitors to google. [CLS] [SEP] the main cookie
used by google analytics is the ‘__ga’ cookie. [CLS] [SEP]

in addition to reporting website usage statistics, google analytics can also
be used, together with some of the advertising cookies described above, to
help show more relevant ads on google properties (like google search) and
across the web and to measure interactions with the ads we show. [CLS] [SEP]

learn more about [analytics cookies and privacy
information](https://developers.google.com/analytics/resources/concepts/gaconceptscookies?hl=en). [CLS] [SEP]

## your choices

  * please note that some cookies, such as processes cookies, are essential to operate our website and without these cookies, the website cannot function properly. [CLS] [SEP]

  * some browsers limit or delete cookies, so you may want to review your cookie settings and [ads settings](https://adssettings.google.com/). [CLS] [SEP] in some browsers you can set up rules to manage cookies on a site-by-site basis, giving you more fine-grained control over your privacy. [CLS] [SEP] this means that you can disallow cookies from all sites except those that you trust. [CLS] [SEP]

  * if you use google chrome browser, you can use the “clear browsing data” option included in tools menu to delete cookies and other site and plug-in data, including data stored on your device by the adobe flash player (commonly known as flash cookies). [CLS] [SEP] see [instructions for managing cookies in chrome](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647). [CLS] [SEP]

  * another feature of chrome is its [incognito mode](https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95464). [CLS] [SEP] you can browse in incognito mode when you don’t want your website visits or downloads to be recorded in your browsing and download histories. [CLS] [SEP] any cookies created while in incognito mode are deleted after you close all incognito windows. [CLS] [SEP]

  * for more information on how you can manage advertising cookies, please see section advertising above. [CLS] [SEP]

  * you can also manage many companies’ cookies used for online advertising via the consumer choice tools created under self-regulation programmes in many countries, such as the us-based [aboutads.info choices](https://www.aboutads.info/choices) page or the eu-based [your online choices](http://www.youronlinechoices.com/uk/your-ad-choices). [CLS] [SEP]

  * finally, you can [manage cookies in your web browser](https://policies.google.com/technologies/managing). [CLS] [SEP]

## changes to this cookie policy

we may update this cookie policy from time to time. [CLS] [SEP] we encourage you to
periodically review this cookie policy to stay informed about our use of
cookies, the information we collect via cookies, and any updates in relation
thereto. [CLS] [SEP] if we make any changes to this cookie policy that significantly
impact the way we use cookies, we will endeavor to provide you with notice in
advance of such change on our website. [CLS] [SEP] your continued use of our website
constitutes your agreement to this cookie policy and any updates. [CLS] [SEP]

## contact us

if you have any questions about our use of cookies, please contact us at
[privacy@waze.com](mailto:privacy@waze.com). [CLS] [SEP]
