# wikihow:cookie policy

##

what are cookies?

cookies are small text files containing a string of characters that can be
placed on your computer or mobile device that uniquely identify your browser
or device. [CLS] [SEP] what are cookies used for?

cookies allow a site or services to know if your computer or device has
visited that site or service before. [CLS] [SEP] cookies can then be used to help
understand how the site or service is being used, help you navigate between
pages efficiently, help remember your preferences, and generally improve your
browsing experience. [CLS] [SEP] cookies can also help ensure marketing you see online is
more relevant to you and your interests. [CLS] [SEP]

##

what types of cookies does wikihow use?

there are generally four categories of cookies: “strictly necessary,”
“performance,” “functionality,” and “targeting.” wikihow routinely uses all
four categories of cookies on the service. [CLS] [SEP] you can find out more about each
cookie category below. [CLS] [SEP]

  1. [CLS] [SEP] strictly necessary cookies. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies are essential, as they enable you to move around the service and use its features, such as accessing logged in or secure areas. [CLS] [SEP]
  2. [CLS] [SEP] performance cookies. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies collect information about how you have used the service, for example, information related to the unique username you have provided, so that less strain is placed on our backend infrastructure. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies may also be used to allow us to know that you have logged in so that we can serve you fresher content than a user who has never logged in. [CLS] [SEP] we also use cookies to track aggregate service usage and experiment with new features and changes on the service. [CLS] [SEP] the information collected is used to improve how the service works. [CLS] [SEP]
  3. [CLS] [SEP] functionality cookies. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies allow us to remember how you’re logged in, whether you made an edit to an article on the service while logged out, when you logged in or out, the state or history of service tools you’ve used. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies also allow us to tailor the service to provide enhanced features and content for you and to remember how you’ve customized the service in other ways, such as customizing the toolbars we offer in the right column of every page. [CLS] [SEP] the information these cookies collect may be anonymous, and they are not used to track your browsing activity on other sites or services. [CLS] [SEP]
  4. [CLS] [SEP] targeting cookies. [CLS] [SEP] wikihow, our advertising partners or other third party partners may use these types of cookies to deliver advertising that is relevant to your interests. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies can remember that your device has visited a site or service, and may also be able to track your device’s browsing activity on other sites or services other than wikihow. [CLS] [SEP] this information may be shared with organizations outside wikihow, such as advertisers and/or advertising networks to deliver the advertising, and to help measure the effectiveness of an advertising campaign, or other business partners for the purpose of providing aggregate service usage statistics and aggregate service testing. [CLS] [SEP]

##

first-party cookies

first-party cookies are cookies that belong to wikihow. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies are used
to enable features on wikihow, such as logging in to the service, and allowing
access to secured tools or pages as described above. [CLS] [SEP]

##

third-party cookies

third-party cookies are cookies that another party places on your device
through our service. [CLS] [SEP] third-party cookies may be placed on your device by
someone providing a service for wikihow, for example to help us understand how
our service is being used. [CLS] [SEP] third-party cookies may also be placed on your
device by our business partners so that they can use them to advertise
products and services to you elsewhere on the internet. [CLS] [SEP] third-party cookies on
wikihow include the following:

  * **google analytics** \- google analytics uses cookies to help us analyze how visitors to wikihow use our website. [CLS] [SEP] google analytics does not receive information from wikihow related to your user name, and your ip address is truncated by google analytics. [CLS] [SEP] data in google analytics can only be viewed in aggregated fashion, and cannot be tied to individual users. [CLS] [SEP] learn more google analytics and privacy from <https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites>. [CLS] [SEP] you can opt out of google analytics tracking via <https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout>. [CLS] [SEP]
  * **google adsense and doubleclick** \- google adsense and doubleclick use cookies to serve advertising on some pages of the wikihow service. [CLS] [SEP] these cookies are be used to customize the advertisements that you see to provide ads that are relevant to you. [CLS] [SEP] visitors in some locations may see ads that are based solely on the content of the page, and that are not based upon any personalized information. [CLS] [SEP] learn more about advertising cookies at <https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads>. [CLS] [SEP]

##

how long will cookies stay on my device?

the length of time a cookie will stay on your computer or mobile device
depends on whether it is a “persistent” or “session” cookie. [CLS] [SEP] session cookies
will only stay on your device until you stop browsing. [CLS] [SEP] persistent cookies stay
on your computer or mobile device until they expire or are deleted. [CLS] [SEP]

##

how to control and delete cookies

if you want to delete cookies follow the instructions at: [how to clear your
browser's cookies](/clear-your-browser%27s-cookies "clear your browser's
cookies"). [CLS] [SEP] if you wish to disable your browser from receiving cookies follow
the instructions at [how to disable cookies](/disable-cookies "disable
cookies"). [CLS] [SEP] note that if you set your browser to disable cookies, you may not
be able to access certain parts of our service and other parts of our service
may not work properly. [CLS] [SEP] you can find out more information cookie settings at
third-party information sites, such as <https://developer.mozilla.org/en-
us/docs/web/http/cookies>. [CLS] [SEP]
