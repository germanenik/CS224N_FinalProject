 end-to-end encryption (e2ee)  it is a method used for securing encrypted data while it is moving from source to the destination. [CLS] [SEP] in end-to-end encryption, the data is encrypted on the sender's system and only the intended recipient will be able to decrypt it. [CLS] [SEP] nobody in between (be they an internet service provider, application service provider or hacker, ...) can read it or tamper with it, thereby providing a great deal of confidentiality and protection to all of your communications.
